Cookie Policy

Effective Date: July 13, 2026

JobsU, Inc. ("JobsU," "we," "our," or "us") uses cookies and similar tracking technologies on our website and platform. This Cookie Policy explains what cookies are, how we use them, what choices you have, and where to find more information. This policy supplements our Privacy Policy.

1. What Are Cookies

Cookies are small text files stored on your computer or mobile device when you visit a website. They are widely used to make websites function properly, remember your preferences, and collect information about how you interact with a site. Similar technologies include web beacons (also called pixels or tracking pixels), local storage, and device fingerprinting.

2. How We Use Cookies

JobsU uses cookies and similar technologies for the following purposes:

  • Authenticating users and maintaining session security
  • Remembering your preferences and settings
  • Understanding how users interact with the platform to improve performance and usability
  • Analyzing website traffic and usage patterns
  • Detecting and preventing fraudulent or abusive activity

3. Types of Cookies We Use

3.1 Essential Cookies

These cookies are necessary for the platform to function. They enable core functionality such as authentication, session management, security protections, and accessibility. Without these cookies, services you have requested (such as signing in to your account) cannot be provided. Essential cookies cannot be disabled.

3.2 Performance and Analytics Cookies

These cookies collect anonymous, aggregated information about how users interact with the platform — such as which pages are visited most often, how long users spend on certain pages, and whether users encounter error messages. This data helps us understand usage patterns and improve the platform. We use PostHog for product analytics.

3.3 Functional Cookies

These cookies allow the platform to remember choices you make (such as your preferred settings or region) and provide enhanced, personalized features. They may be set by us or by third-party providers whose services we have integrated into our pages.

5. Cookie Retention

Different cookies have different lifespans:

  • Session Cookies: Automatically deleted when you close your browser. Used for temporary authentication and session management.
  • Persistent Cookies: Remain on your device until they expire or you manually delete them. Used for remembering preferences and login state.
  • Authentication Cookies: Typically expire after 30 days of inactivity or upon logout, whichever comes first.

6. Managing Your Cookie Preferences

6.1 Browser Settings

Most web browsers allow you to view, manage, and delete cookies through their settings, typically under "Privacy," "Security," or "Cookies" in your browser's preferences menu:

  • Safari: Preferences > Privacy > Manage Website Data
  • Chrome: Settings > Privacy and Security > Cookies and Other Site Data
  • Firefox: Settings > Privacy & Security > Cookies and Site Data
  • Edge: Settings > Cookies and Site Permissions > Cookies and Site Data

6.2 Opt-Out Tools

For analytics cookies, you can opt out using industry tools provided by the following organizations:

  • Digital Advertising Alliance: aboutads.info/choices
  • Network Advertising Initiative: networkadvertising.org/choices

6.3 Do Not Track

Some browsers offer a "Do Not Track" (DNT) signal. The platform does not currently respond to DNT signals, but we respect your choices through the cookie management options described above.

7. Impact of Disabling Cookies

If you disable or reject cookies, some features of the platform may not function properly — for example, you may need to sign in each time you visit, your preferences may not be remembered, and certain interactive features may not work as expected. Essential cookies cannot be disabled through cookie preference settings because they are required for the platform to function.

8. Advertising

JobsU does not currently use advertising or targeting cookies on the platform. We do not serve third-party advertisements and we do not sell your personal information to advertisers. If this changes in the future, we will update this Cookie Policy and obtain your consent where required by applicable law.

9. Changes to This Cookie Policy

We may update this Cookie Policy from time to time to reflect changes in our practices, technology, or legal requirements. When we make changes, we will post the updated policy on the platform with a new effective date. For significant changes, we will notify users through email or in-app notification where practicable.
