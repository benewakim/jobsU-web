FAQ

Common questions

Everything you need to know before you sign up.

When does the app launch, and which schools are included?

We're launching in Boston, MA soon — starting with Boston College, Harvard, BU, Northeastern, Tufts, and Wellesley. More Boston-area schools will follow. Join the waitlist and we'll email you the moment it's live.

How do students get paid?

Students connect a bank account through the app (powered by Stripe) and receive direct deposits after each completed job. No job on JobsU pays less than minimum wage — the rate is always visible before you apply.

Is it safe? How are students verified?

Every student account requires a valid .edu email address, tying each profile to a real enrolled student. No anonymous profiles — you always know who's showing up. Both parties rate each other after every job, and the app has built-in reporting and blocking tools.

How much does it cost to post a job?

Posting a job is free during our launch period. We'll share pricing details before any fees go live — you'll never be charged without notice.

What kinds of jobs can I post or find?

Babysitting, yard work, moving help, pet care, errands, tech help, in-person tutoring, and more. If it's a local task a college student can help with, it belongs on JobsU.
